## Overview

pH standards define the acceptable pH range for products that require pH testing. Not all products require pH measurement.

<Warning>
These standards are extracted from `lib/production-constants.ts:222-242` and represent the source of truth for pH quality control.
</Warning>

## Validation Range

All pH measurements must be between **0 and 14**.

## Products WITHOUT pH Requirements

The following product categories do **not** require pH measurement:

- **Aromatizantes Ambientales** (all ARO* products)
- **Bases Aromatizantes** (all BARO* products)
- **Limpiadores Multiusos** (most LIM* products)
- **Suavizantes de Telas** (all SUA* products)
- **Reforzadores de Aroma** (all REFARO-* products)
- **Some intermediate products** (CLO, INS, LIMVID, etc.)

See the [Parameter Applicability](/reference/parameter-applicability) reference for the complete matrix.

## pH by Product Category

### Personal Care Products (pH 5-7)
Personal care products like hand soaps, shampoos, and creams typically have acidic to neutral pH to match skin pH.

### Laundry Detergents (pH 7-9)
Laundry detergents are slightly alkaline to help break down oils and stains.

### Dish Detergents (pH 7-9)
Dish washing detergents are neutral to slightly alkaline for effective grease cutting.

### Automotive Products (pH 6-7)
Automotive products maintain neutral pH to avoid damage to vehicle surfaces.

## Conformity Analysis

Unlike solids measurements, pH conformity is typically binary:

```typescript
if (ph >= phStandard.min && ph <= phStandard.max) {
  return 'CONFORME'
} else {
  return 'NO CONFORME'
}
```

<Info>
pH measurements do not use the ±5% tolerance zone that applies to solids measurements.
</Info>
